"Intelligent Transport Systems and Services"


Intelligent Transport Systems and Services (ITS) are advanced applications aimed at providing innovative services related to different modes of transport (road, rail, sea and air) in order to, on the one hand, facilitating mobility, making transport use safer, more coordinated and "smarter" and, on the other hand, reducing energy consumption and CO2 emissions, helping to improve environmental conditions.

The communication revolution of the 1980s and the growing importance of interoperability and technology integration have contributed to the development of new, innovative transport systems and services. Intelligent Transport Systems and Services include telematics, namely all types of vehicle communications, between vehicles (eg car-cars) and between vehicles and fixed locations (eg car-infrastructure). However, their use is not restricted to road transport, they also include the use of information and communication technologies (ICT) in rail, sea and air transport, and can be used by operators and regulators, passengers and also cargo carriers .

The ITS applications, for which the Electrical and Computer Engineering has played a decisive role, become a characteristic and an integral part of the "fabric" of transport.
